Hong Shao Rou

Description

Hong Shao Rou (红烧肉) is a sticky, sweet, aromatic and umami braised meat dish that's a classic across China. Similar dishes exist in many Asian countries, but the key ingredients for an authentic Chinese version include rock sugar, dark soy sauce, star anise and Xhaoxing wine.

Ingredients

  1. 2 pounds skin-on pork belly
  2. 4 tablespoons rock sugar
  3. 3 tablespoons light soy sauce
  4. 1/4 cup Shaoxing wine
  5. 1/3 cup dark soy sauce
  6. 2 inch knob ginger
  7. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  8. 4-6 dried Chinese chili peppers

Steps

  • Add pork belly to a pot, cover with water and bring to a boil. After 5 minutes, skim any impurities, remove the pork belly and reserve the blanching liquid.
  • Wash the pork belly. Cut the pork belly into one inch chunks and set aside.
  • Wash the pot before slowly heating the rock sugar on low in oil until it's dissolved and creates a dark brown caramel.
  • Brown the pork belly pieces on all sides in the caramel. Depending on the size of the pot, you may need to do this in batches.
  • Once all of the meat has been browned, add it all back in. Add in the reserved blanching liquid, along with all of the other ingredients.
  • Cover the pot and wait for it to boil. Keep it on a low simmer for 90-120 minutes. The pork is reaady when you can easily pass a chopstick through the meat with little resistance.
  • If the braising liquid is not thick enough to coat a spoon, you may remove the meat to further thicken the sauce prior to serving.
